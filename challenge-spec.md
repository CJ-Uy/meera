## Problem

University service desks—particularly IT—are overwhelmed by a high volume of repetitive student requests such as Wi-Fi access issues, account lockouts, tuition balance questions, registration holds, and printer outages. Today, most requests arrive through unstructured emails or forms. Regardless of simplicity, every request requires a staff member to manually read the message, request missing identifiers (student ID, course term, device type), and then log a ticket into a system.

This manual intake process creates operational bottlenecks. Simple requests waste skilled staff time, while genuinely urgent issues (e.g., system access failures during exams or enrollment deadlines) face delays of up to two days, negatively impacting student experience and institutional efficiency.

---

## Proposed Solution

Design an intelligent, agent-driven service entry point for university support that prioritizes resolution over routing.

Rather than acting as a static form or chatbot, the agent should autonomously interpret a student's vague or emotional request, resolve issues when possible, using institutional knowledge, and prepare a high-fidelity, structured case package when human escalation is required.

The agent functions as a logic-driven partner across university operations, capable of pre-processing and triaging requests that span multiple departments while maintaining clear ownership and escalation boundaries.

## User Roles

### Students (Primary Users)

Students are expected to:
- Initiate support requests using natural language without selecting a department
- Provide identifying details (e.g., email, student ID) when prompted
- Follow agent-guided troubleshooting steps for common issues
- Confirm whether the issue has been resolved
- Accept that some requests will require human departmental follow-up

### IT Department

Responsible for:
- Resolving technical issues such as network access, system outages, devices, and printers
- Receiving pre-classified, fully populated tickets from the agent
- Avoiding repetitive intake tasks due to agent-collected context
- Focusing on issues that require hands-on technical expertise

### Registrar

Responsible for:
- Addressing academic records, enrollment issues, registration holds, and term-based queries
- Receiving tickets already tagged with relevant academic context (term, status, urgency)
- Leveraging the agent to deflect repetitive, FAQ-level questions

### Medical / Campus Health Services

Responsible for:
- Handling health-related administrative inquiries (appointment scheduling, documentation guidance, service access)
- Receiving escalations only when student needs exceed available FAQ guidance
- Maintaining confidentiality and policy compliance beyond the agent's scope

### Student Services

Responsible for:
- Supporting student life concerns (ID access, campus services, facilities, general support requests)
- Using the agent as a first line FAQ responder and intake assistant
- Receiving structured tickets only when human intervention is needed

---

## Agentic AI Role

The Agentic AI acts as a cross-departmental front door that:
- Interprets student intent without requiring department selection
- Determines whether an issue can be resolved via FAQs or guidance
- Asks targeted follow-up questions only when necessary
- Extracts and structures key entities (identity, issue type, urgency)
- Routes unresolved issues to the correct department with full context
- Prevents unnecessary tickets by resolving common questions autonomously
- Maintains strict boundaries—no policy decisions or system overrides

The agent's purpose is to reduce ticket volume, improve triage quality, and accelerate resolution, not to replace departmental staff.
