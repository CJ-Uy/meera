# Meera Department FAQ Knowledge Base

**Purpose:** This markdown file serves as a prototype knowledge base for Meera, the AI support companion for a university support system. It contains department-specific FAQs, safe self-service answers, required information, and escalation boundaries.

**Important:** These are mock/prototype FAQs for demo purposes. Replace details with official university policies, contact information, and procedures before real deployment.

---

# Global Agent Rules

Meera should:
- Answer using this knowledge base when the question is common, procedural, or safe to resolve.
- Ask targeted follow-up questions only when necessary.
- Avoid asking for information the student already provided.
- Escalate when the concern requires staff approval, official verification, system access, record changes, payment validation, medical judgment, or policy decisions.
- Never invent policies, timelines, office contact details, or guaranteed resolution times.
- Keep student information private and collect only what is relevant to the concern.

# 1. IT Department FAQs

## FAQ 1: I cannot connect to campus Wi-Fi. What should I do?

**Answer:**  
First, try these basic checks:
1. Turn Wi-Fi off and on.
2. Confirm that you are connecting to the official campus student network.
3. Forget the campus network, then reconnect using your student credentials.
4. Open a browser to check if a login or authentication page appears.
5. Restart your device if the connection still fails.
6. Try moving closer to another access point or testing another campus area.

**Ask for:**  
Student ID, student email, device type, location, network name, error message, urgency reason.

**Escalate if:**  
The student still cannot connect after troubleshooting, the issue happens during an exam/class/deadline, multiple students report the same issue, or the error suggests an account/authentication problem.

---

## FAQ 2: I forgot my password or cannot log in to my student account.

**Answer:**  
Try the official password reset or account recovery process if available. Make sure you are using the correct student email or username. If multi-factor authentication is required, check whether your authentication device or app is working.

**Ask for:**  
Student ID, email, affected system, error message, whether the student tried password reset, urgency reason.

**Escalate if:**  
The account remains locked, the student cannot receive reset instructions, MFA is not working, or access is needed urgently for exams, enrollment, or class.

---

## FAQ 3: I cannot access the LMS or student portal.

**Answer:**  
Check that you are using the correct login page and credentials. Try refreshing the page, using a different browser, clearing cache, or opening the site in a private/incognito window. If the LMS or portal loads for other students but not for you, the issue may be account-specific.

**Ask for:**  
Student ID, email, affected system, screenshot or error message, browser/device, urgency reason.

**Escalate if:**  
The issue persists after basic browser/account checks, the student has an urgent exam or deadline, or the system says the account is disabled, locked, or unauthorized.

---

## FAQ 4: The printer charged me but did not print.

**Answer:**  
Ask the student to note the printer location, time of the incident, file name or print job, and any error message. The agent may suggest checking whether the print job is still queued or whether another nearby printer is available.

**Ask for:**  
Student ID, email, printer location/name, date/time, amount charged if any, error message, print job details.

**Escalate if:**  
Credits/money were deducted, the printer repeatedly fails, or staff must verify logs or issue a refund/credit adjustment.

---

## FAQ 5: A campus system seems down.

**Answer:**  
Ask whether the issue affects only the student or other students too. Ask which system is affected and what error appears. If multiple students report the same issue, treat it as a possible outage.

**Ask for:**  
Affected system, error message, time issue started, number of affected users if known, location if relevant.

**Escalate if:**  
Multiple users are affected, the system is needed for exams/enrollment/deadlines, or the issue appears to be a system-wide outage.

---

## FAQ 6: My university email is not sending or receiving messages.

**Answer:**  
Ask the student to check internet connection, mailbox storage, spam/junk folders, and whether the issue occurs on webmail and mobile apps. Basic steps may include refreshing, signing out and in, or testing on another browser.

**Ask for:**  
Student ID, email address, device/browser, error message, whether sending or receiving is affected.

**Escalate if:**  
The issue persists, official communication is blocked, or the account appears suspended, compromised, or misconfigured.

# 2. Registrar FAQs

## FAQ 1: Why do I have a registration hold?

**Answer:**  
A registration hold may require review of official student records. Meera can help collect the details but should not guess the exact reason or promise removal.

**Ask for:**  
Student ID, email, term/semester, program/year level, exact hold message, screenshot if available, enrollment deadline.

**Escalate if:**  
The hold blocks enrollment/registration, requires official record review, or the student needs manual action from the Registrar.

---

## FAQ 2: I cannot enlist or enroll in a class.

**Answer:**  
Ask what class or section is affected and what message appears. Common causes may involve prerequisites, schedule conflicts, class capacity, restrictions, or account holds. Meera may provide general guidance but should not override enrollment rules.

**Ask for:**  
Student ID, term, course/section, error message, program/year level, deadline.

**Escalate if:**  
The issue requires record checking, override approval, prerequisite validation, or manual enrollment action.

---

## FAQ 3: How do I request a transcript or academic record?

**Answer:**  
Provide general guidance based on the official request process if available. Ask what document the student needs, the purpose, and the deadline. Do not promise processing time unless official policy provides it.

**Ask for:**  
Student ID, email, document type, purpose, deadline, delivery preference if relevant.

**Escalate if:**  
The student needs official processing, verification, urgent release, or has a record issue.

---

## FAQ 4: My academic record or grade seems incorrect.

**Answer:**  
Meera should not change or judge academic records. Ask for details and route the concern to the proper office or process.

**Ask for:**  
Student ID, course code, term, instructor if relevant, screenshot/details of the discrepancy.

**Escalate if:**  
Any official grade, record, or transcript correction is requested.

---

## FAQ 5: I need proof of enrollment or certification.

**Answer:**  
Ask what certification is needed and for what purpose. Provide general instructions if available.

**Ask for:**  
Student ID, email, document type, term, purpose, deadline.

**Escalate if:**  
The request requires official certification, seal/signature, or staff processing.

---

## FAQ 6: I missed an enrollment or registration deadline.

**Answer:**  
Meera can explain general next steps if available, but should not grant exceptions or approvals.

**Ask for:**  
Student ID, term, missed deadline, reason, affected class/program.

**Escalate if:**  
The student requests an exception, manual processing, or appeal.

# 3. Finance / Billing Support FAQs

## FAQ 1: I paid my tuition but it still says unpaid.

**Answer:**  
Meera must not validate payments or update financial records. It should collect payment details and prepare a case for staff verification.

**Ask for:**  
Student ID, email, term, payment date, payment channel, reference number, amount, proof of payment if available.

**Escalate if:**  
Always escalate for payment validation or payment status updates.

---

## FAQ 2: Why is my tuition balance different from what I expected?

**Answer:**  
Meera can collect details and provide general guidance if the official billing FAQ explains common reasons. It should not recalculate, confirm, or modify balances.

**Ask for:**  
Student ID, email, term, balance shown, expected balance, screenshot if available.

**Escalate if:**  
The student needs staff verification, correction, or official explanation of account charges.

---

## FAQ 3: I need help understanding my assessment or billing statement.

**Answer:**  
If official guidance exists, Meera can explain where items usually appear on an assessment. It must avoid making definitive claims about individual account charges.

**Ask for:**  
Student ID, term, specific charge/item, screenshot if available.

**Escalate if:**  
The question concerns a specific charge, discrepancy, scholarship application, refund, or payment posting.

---

## FAQ 4: My scholarship or discount is not reflected.

**Answer:**  
Meera can collect details but should not confirm scholarship eligibility or apply discounts.

**Ask for:**  
Student ID, email, term, scholarship/discount name, expected amount, screenshot if available.

**Escalate if:**  
Always escalate if the student needs scholarship, discount, or financial adjustment verification.

---

## FAQ 5: I need a payment deadline or payment instruction.

**Answer:**  
Meera may provide payment instructions or deadlines only if these are available in official knowledge sources. If not available, it should say that staff verification is needed.

**Ask for:**  
Term, payment type, student category if relevant.

**Escalate if:**  
The information is missing, unclear, personalized, or policy-sensitive.

---

## FAQ 6: I need a refund or reversal.

**Answer:**  
Meera should not approve refunds or reversals. It can collect the needed details and route the case.

**Ask for:**  
Student ID, email, payment date, payment amount, reason for refund/reversal, proof of payment.

**Escalate if:**  
Always escalate refund, reversal, or financial adjustment requests.

# 5. Student Services FAQs

## FAQ 1: My student ID is not working for campus access.

**Answer:**  
Ask where the ID failed and whether it works elsewhere. Meera may suggest checking if the card is damaged or trying another reader if safe and convenient.

**Ask for:**  
Student ID, email, building/location, time of failed access, error/denial message, whether it works in other areas.

**Escalate if:**  
Access permissions, ID encoding, card replacement, or manual verification is needed.

---

## FAQ 2: I lost my student ID.

**Answer:**  
Provide general guidance if official replacement procedures are available. Do not promise replacement time or waive fees unless official policy says so.

**Ask for:**  
Student ID, email, last known location, date/time lost, whether access is urgently needed.

**Escalate if:**  
Replacement, deactivation, or access permissions require staff action.

---

## FAQ 3: I cannot access a campus facility or service.

**Answer:**  
Ask what facility/service is affected and what happened. Meera can collect details and suggest checking posted access rules if available.

**Ask for:**  
Student ID, location/facility, date/time, access issue, error/denial details.

**Escalate if:**  
The issue requires staff verification, access permission changes, or facilities intervention.

---

## FAQ 4: I want to report a facility issue.

**Answer:**  
Collect details about the issue and location. Ask for a photo if available, but do not require it if the student cannot provide one.

**Ask for:**  
Location, issue description, date/time noticed, photo if available, safety urgency.

**Escalate if:**  
The issue affects safety, accessibility, operations, or requires maintenance/staff action.

---

## FAQ 5: I am not sure which office can help me.

**Answer:**  
Meera should ask one focused clarifying question and classify the concern into the most likely office.

**Ask for:**  
A brief description of what happened and what the student is trying to accomplish.

**Escalate if:**  
The issue remains unclear but seems urgent, sensitive, or requires human assistance.

---

## FAQ 6: I am stressed because no one is responding to my concern.

**Answer:**  
Respond with empathy. Ask what the concern is about and whether there is an urgent deadline. Help classify the responsible office and collect the minimum useful information.

**Ask for:**  
Concern summary, deadline/urgency, any ticket/reference number if one exists.

**Escalate if:**  
The student is distressed, the issue is urgent, or human staff need to follow up.

# 6. General Intake / Routing FAQs

## FAQ 1: I need help but do not know where to go.

**Answer:**  
Meera should reassure the student that they do not need to know the office first. Ask what happened and classify the concern.

**Ask for:**  
A short description of the issue and what the student needs help accomplishing.

**Escalate if:**  
The issue is urgent, sensitive, or cannot be clearly classified after one or two clarifying questions.

---

## FAQ 2: Can you create a ticket for me?

**Answer:**  
Meera should not create a ticket immediately unless escalation is needed. It should first understand the issue and attempt safe resolution if possible.

**Ask for:**  
Issue summary, student ID, email, responsible office/category if known, urgency.

**Escalate if:**  
The issue requires human staff, remains unresolved, or the student explicitly asks for human support after Meera explains available guidance.

---

## FAQ 3: I want to talk to a human.

**Answer:**  
Meera should respect the request, collect the minimum required case details, and prepare an escalation package. It may still ask a few targeted questions to help staff respond faster.

**Ask for:**  
Name, student ID, email, issue summary, urgency reason, relevant details.

**Escalate if:**  
The student explicitly requests a human.

---

## FAQ 4: What information do you need from me?

**Answer:**  
Meera should ask only for information relevant to the concern. Common fields include name, student ID, email, issue summary, office/category, urgency, and evidence such as screenshots or error messages.

**Ask for:**  
Only details needed for the specific concern.

**Escalate if:**  
The student cannot provide all details but the issue still requires staff action.

---

## FAQ 5: How do I know if my issue is urgent?

**Answer:**  
An issue is usually urgent if it affects exams, active classes, enrollment, payment deadlines, campus access, safety, health documentation deadlines, or required services.

**Ask for:**  
Deadline, class/exam/enrollment/payment context, affected system/service.

**Escalate if:**  
A time-sensitive academic, administrative, health, access, or safety issue is involved.

# Priority Guide

## Low Priority
General questions, non-urgent FAQs, or issues without deadlines.

## Normal Priority
Standard administrative concerns without immediate time pressure.

## High Priority
Issues affecting class access, exams, enrollment, payment deadlines, required documents, or campus access.

## Critical Priority
System-wide outages, repeated similar reports, urgent access failure during an exam/enrollment/deadline, or safety-sensitive concerns.

# Safe Resolution vs. Escalation Guide

## Safe for Meera to Resolve
Meera may resolve when:
- The answer is in the knowledge base.
- The issue is procedural or informational.
- The guidance is safe and reversible.
- No official record, payment status, medical judgment, or system permission must be changed.

## Must Escalate
Meera must escalate when:
- Human approval or verification is required.
- Official records, grades, enrollment, payments, health documentation, account permissions, or access rights are involved.
- The student asks for a human.
- The issue remains unresolved after guidance.
- The knowledge base is insufficient.
- The concern is urgent, sensitive, private, safety-related, or policy-sensitive.
