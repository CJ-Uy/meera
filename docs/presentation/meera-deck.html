Meera.

The agentic AI front door for university support — built for resolution over routing.

Demo Day · Microsoft Philippines · June 15, 2026

MEERA · 01
01 · The Problem

The front door of support is a form —
and forms can't think.

Unstructured requests

Students describe issues in their own words — no department, no urgency, no system details. Wi-Fi outages, lockouts, holds, balances, printers.

Manual intake

Every request — however simple — needs a staff member to read it, chase the student ID, course term, and device details, then log a ticket by hand.

Delayed urgency

Genuinely urgent issues — access failures during exams or enrollment — wait up to two days behind routine questions.

Skilled staff time goes to intake, not resolution. Students wait. Everyone loses.

MEERA · 02
02 · The Idea

Every concern ends one of exactly two ways.

① Safe self-service resolution

Knowledge-base-backed guidance fixes the issue on the spot. No ticket ever exists.

② Staff-ready escalation package

Classified, prioritized, complete with collected context. Staff start at "resolve," never at "intake."

  • No department selection. Students just describe what's wrong — vague, emotional, typos and all.
  • Strict boundaries. Never approves, never edits records, never validates payments, never makes medical judgments, never overrides systems.
  • A partner to staff — not a replacement.
MEERA · 03
03 · How Meera Works

One agent loop behind every conversation.

1

Understand

Interprets natural-language concerns, even vague or emotional ones.

2

Classify

Identifies the responsible office, category, urgency, and missing details.

3

Resolve

Approved knowledge guides safe self-service steps before staff time is used.

4

Package

Prepares a structured escalation when human support is needed.

5

Route

The right department — or several at once — receives a complete, prioritized case.

ITRegistrarFinance / BillingMedical / Campus HealthStudent Services

Five departments live today, each with its own knowledge base and escalation rules. Every reply in this demo is a live LLM call with function calling — nothing is scripted.

MEERA · 06
05 · Creativity — Battle Mode

The same real AI session —
re-skinned as a battle.

  • Your problem is the boss. Each department maps to a meerkat predator — the default boss is the cobra. Multi-department issues spawn a pack.
  • Moves are AI-suggested replies rendered as quick-reply buttons; free typing always works.
  • Enemy HP = case progress. Two victories: issue self-resolved (boss defeated) or ticket filed (backup called). The student never loses.
  • Identical engine. Same gateway, same ticket pipeline as the classic chat — one data model, two experiences.
MEERA · 07
06 · Creativity — Desktop Guidance

Some problems need pointing, not paragraphs.

  • Electron desktop assistant draws transparent, click-through overlays over any application: guiding cursor, arrows, highlights, instruction bubbles.
  • Vision-grounded. Meera captures the screen and a multimodal model locates the actual control to point at.
  • Zoom-refine grounding: a second pass crops and upscales around the first guess, re-locates the target, and maps back to full-screen coordinates.
  • One validated protocol. Simulator and AI emit the same typed commands; coordinates normalized 0–1, multi-monitor aware.
MEERA · 08
07 · The Staff Side

Tickets that arrive ready to resolve. ▶ live demo

  • Pre-processed tickets: AI summary, collected information, missing information, suggested actions, and a confidence score.
  • Priority matrix: every ticket plotted by severity × complexity for instant visual triage.
  • Claiming, notes, status flow — New → In progress → Awaiting student → Resolved.
  • Cross-department escalation: invite another office, they accept or reject with a reason, a shared task board coordinates the work.
MEERA · 09
08 · Knowledge That Compounds

Every resolved ticket makes the next one cheaper.

  • Knowledge graph, not a flat FAQ list — FAQs, procedures, entities, and departments as nodes with typed relationships.
  • One-click ingestion turns a resolved ticket into a reusable KB entry.
  • Insights dashboard: opened vs. resolved volume, severity × complexity heatmap, team load, response & resolution times, AI-handled share.
  • KB coverage gaps: clusters of low-confidence tickets tell staff exactly which article to write next.
MEERA · 10
09 · Data Model Design

Nine tables. Many experiences. Zero redesigns.

TableRole in the system
ticketsThe agent's work product as first-class columns: aiSummary · collectedInformation · missingInformation · suggestedActions · confidence, plus severity × complexity enums that power the priority matrix.
ticket_messagesStudent ↔ Meera transcript, one row per turn.
ticket_notesInternal staff discussion — never mixed with the student transcript.
cross_dept_participantsJunction table: ticket × department with accept/reject decision + reason.
tasksShared cross-department work items with owner, assignee, status, due date.
kb_nodes / kb_edgesKnowledge as a graph — faq, procedure, entity, department nodes with typed relations.
users / adminsStudents and department staff (IT · REG · FIN · MED · SS).
  • Drizzle ORM on Cloudflare D1 (SQLite), enum-constrained at the schema level.
  • Graph KB lets Meera reason about relationships — e.g., registration hold depends on payment clearance.
  • The same schema serves the student chat, Battle Mode, five admin dashboards, and the analytics.
MEERA · 11
10 · Architecture & Trust

One codebase, three targets, hard boundaries.

App

Next.js 16 · React 19 · Tailwind v4 · shadcn/ui. Runs in the browser, as an Electron desktop app, and on Cloudflare Workers in production (D1 + R2). Runtime-keyed adapters pick the backend per environment.

AI

Groq inference: Llama 3.1 8B for text, Llama 4 Scout for vision, Whisper (Workers AI) for voice. All calls server-side — the browser never sees a credential. Tool calls validated before touching the DB or screen.

Boundaries

No approvals · no record changes · no payment validation · no medical judgments · no system overrides. Enforced in the system prompt and the tool design — sensitive actions are forced down the escalation path.

Prototyped in Microsoft Copilot Studio; the institutional deployment path integrates Teams handoffs, SharePoint knowledge bases, Power Automate routing, and Dataverse records.

MEERA · 12
11 · Business Impact

Less volume. Better triage. Faster resolution.

Deflect

Tickets that never exist. Safe self-service resolves the repetitive majority — Wi-Fi, lockouts, FAQ-level questions — before they consume staff time.

Zero intake

Complete on arrival. Classified, prioritized, context-collected tickets erase the read–chase–log cycle and the two-day urgent-issue delay with it.

Triage

Urgency surfaces instantly. Severity + deadline awareness moves the student locked out during finals to the front of the queue automatically.

Compound

The system gets cheaper weekly. Resolved tickets feed the knowledge graph; coverage-gap analytics say which article to write next.

And it travels: the same understand → classify → resolve → package → route pattern fits HR, finance ops, facilities, and ITSM. The university is the beachhead, not the ceiling.
